Everyone’s different — including you. That’s why at ALU, we don’t do “one size fits all” education. ALU’s Leadership Core program and the first year of real work experience will prepare you for our unique approach to individualized learning.

We build the skills that you, as a young leader and entrepreneur will need for the future. Harness the characteristics essential for creating a mission-based life of impact and purpose. All ALU students develop 21st-century skills and network that will remain relevant amidst rapid global change. Want to guide that change? With the right education, you can.

Interact with our Computing Students

Nathan

Year 4

I believe that the Computing degree at ALC prepares you very much for certain aspects of what life can and will be like as a software developer. You’ll find that in as much as the course content may hold information relevant to completing your assignments, you’ll always stand to gain more by being more assertive and taking initiative in doing your own research. There will be situations in which relying on the content alone is not enough. You’ll learn to be independent, to do your own research for your own betterment. You learn to take control of your own academic improvement. And that is extremely valuable.

Rudo Togara

Cloud Solutions Specialist

Before I started studying Computing, I had a fixed mindset about what it entailed. I had very little knowledge of all the opportunities that are within this department and I am grateful that now, I am becoming aware of them. Not only have I come to know these opportunities but I have also become part of the whole innovation around the science of computing. With every project I work on, I learn and I use that to show other people the possibilities that are created within this department.

Cynthia Iraduka

Software Architect

What I like best about the computing degree is the possibility to first explore the different fields in tech.
